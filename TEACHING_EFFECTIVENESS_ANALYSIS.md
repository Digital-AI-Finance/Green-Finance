### Recommendations: Add Active Elements

**After Slide 8 (Market Segmentation):**
```latex
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Think-Pair-Share (2 minutes)]
\textbf{Question:} Why can't conventional investors arbitrage away the greenium by buying green bonds and selling them to ESG investors?

\textbf{Discuss with partner, then share with class.}

\textit{Key insight: Segmentation limits arbitrage due to preference-based utility.}
\end{tcolorbox}
```

**After Slide 18 (Regional Chart):**
```latex
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Quick Calculation (1 minute)]
Given: Total market = \$2.9T, Asia-Pacific = 27\%

\textbf{Calculate:} Dollar value of Asia-Pacific market

\textbf{Your answer:} \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ billion USD

\textit{Solution: \$2,900B × 0.27 = \$783B}
\end{tcolorbox}
```

**After Slide 20 (Instrument Breakdown):**
```latex
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Data Interpretation (1 minute)]
Chart shows: Green bonds 76\% (\$1.6T), SLBs 24\% (\$500B)

\textbf{Calculate:} Does 76\% of \$2.9T = \$1.6T? (Check data consistency)

\textbf{Poll:} Why might SLBs be growing faster than green bonds?
\end{tcolorbox}
```

**After Slide 27 (Greenium Pricing):**
```latex
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Conceptual Check (30 seconds)]
\textbf{Quick Poll:} If greenium increases from 3 bps to 10 bps, green bond price:

\begin{itemize}
\item[$\circ$] Goes UP (higher demand)
\item[$\circ$] Goes DOWN (higher required return)
\item[$\circ$] Stays same (no effect)
\end{itemize}

\textit{Correct: UP - Lower discount rate $(r-g)$ increases PV of cash flows}
\end{tcolorbox}
```

**After Slide 34 (Greenium Decline):**
```latex
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Economic Reasoning (2 minutes)]
\textbf{Question:} Chart shows greenium declined from 7 bps (2019) to 2 bps (2024). Using segmentation theory from Slide 8, explain TWO economic forces causing this decline.

\textbf{Hint:} Think about supply and demand dynamics.

\textit{Answer: (1) Green bond supply increased, (2) ESG demand partially satisfied}
\end{tcolorbox}
```
